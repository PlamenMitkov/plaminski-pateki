EcoProject
Assistant Architecture Refactor Report
Date: 2026-03-21
Scope: assistant service modularization + maintainability hardening

Summary
Implemented a targeted architectural refactor in the assistant layer to reduce coupling in OpenAiAssistantService and isolate security/data-governance/retrieval/enrichment/provider-transport/provider-fallback/weather-context concerns into dedicated services.

Architecture Impact
- Reduced responsibility concentration in OpenAiAssistantService.
- Improved separation of concerns between:
  - orchestration flow
  - prompt security policy
  - provenance/trust policy
  - retrieval and alternative recommendation policy
  - enrichment workflow execution
  - provider transport and retry/error handling
  - provider fallback policy/routing decisions
  - weather prompt/context logic
  - session/chat orchestration and access-control flow
- Increased testability of policy logic as independent components.
- Lowered change risk for future governance/security iterations.

Known Limitations
- OpenAiAssistantService remains a central orchestrator with many dependencies, although major concerns are now extracted.
- Next safe step is splitting high-level chat-response assembly into a dedicated response-composition pipeline service.

Recommended Next Step (Incremental)
P1:
- Extract high-level response pipeline sequencing (provider call + post-processing + telemetry) behind a dedicated application workflow service.
- Add focused unit tests for provider fallback disablement scenarios (Gemini failure with policy deny, OpenAI secondary fallback deny) to validate strict no-fallback behavior when policy gates are closed.
